Work Experience


Panasonic Avionics | Certification Engineering Intern

June 2019 - August 2019

In my time at Panasonic Avionics, if there is anything I learned, it was how much work goes into a design after it is seen as complete. All of the work that goes into qualification and compliance testing in order for a product to be considered safe to use, makes me appreciate complicated projects even more.

While there, I spent most of my time testing Line Replacement Units (LRUs), such as server cabinets in passenger liners, to make sure they are compliant with FAA and manufacturer standards. A complete overview of my responsibilities can be seen below:

  • Conduct Structural, ENV, Smoke/Leak, and Cooling tests and identify failures
  • Generate Flammability, Structural, ENV, Smoke/Leak, Cooling, and ATP test reports
  • Work with various engineering groups (Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering & Electro-Magnetic Interference) to help evaluate selection of new materials for LRUs that are compliant with FAA, EASA, and OEM requirements

JPL MALTO Student-Redesign | Researcher Assistant

February 2019 - Current

In cooperation with the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, Cal Poly Pomona set out to design a new user interface for the JPL in-house program MALTO. MALTO, also known as Mission Analysis for Low Thrust Optimization, is a medium fidelity mission planning tool used primarily for optimizing trajectories involve low thrust engines such as solar sails or ion engines. Cal Poly Pomona's goal is to make a new GUI that facilitates easier use over what program currently offers, with the end goal being MALTO's inclusion into orbital dynamics courses.

Throughout the project, I have been contributing to both the "Frontend" and the "Backend" of the design. In the current Alpha version, I developed a skeleton/boilerplate code/format that the rest of the GUI was based off of. It allowed for a modular design to that could add new features as users progress in learning the program (or as they are added to the backend itself). For pictures and example use cases see below.
